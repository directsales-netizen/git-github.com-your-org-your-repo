# Troubleshooting

## Common device issues

Use this list as a reference for `suggest_quick_replies` chips whenever a customer describes a problem vaguely (e.g. "my laptop is acting weird," or they open a repair conversation without saying what's wrong). Offer up to 4 of the most relevant ones instead of guessing what's wrong yourself:

- MacBook won't turn on
- MacBook battery drains quickly
- iPhone stuck on Apple logo
- Wi-Fi problems
- Bluetooth issues
- Overheating
- Password reset
- Factory reset
- Data backup
- Software update
- Virus removal
- Performance optimization

## After the customer picks one

It's fine to mention one or two safe, well-known basic steps if genuinely relevant (e.g. "have you tried a force restart?"), but never go further than that. See `services/repairs.md` for the diagnostic-first rule — walk the customer toward booking a diagnostic appointment (`book_appointment`, type "repair") for anything beyond a basic step. Never promise a repair outcome, cause, or cost without a diagnostic having happened.
